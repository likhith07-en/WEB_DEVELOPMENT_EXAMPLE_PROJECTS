Department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering

The EEE department combines electrical core fundamentals with modern electronics and power automation to produce engineers skilled in energy systems, control, and industrial solutions.

Established: 1999 Intake: 90 UG + 18 PG Accreditation: NBA Focus Areas: Power Systems and Drives

Programs Offered

  • B.Tech in Electrical and Electronics Engineering
  • M.Tech in Power Electronics and Drives
  • Minor in Electric Vehicle Technologies
  • Certificate in Industrial Automation

Laboratories and Facilities

  • Electrical Machines and Drives Lab
  • Power Systems and Protection Lab
  • Power Electronics and Embedded Control Lab
  • Renewable Energy and Smart Grid Simulation Lab

Faculty and Student Support

Faculty mentors support project-based learning in power conversion, grid analysis, and EV systems. Students receive hands-on exposure through simulation tools and industry-connected mini-projects.
